Understanding your hair's porosity is the first step to healthier hair. Here's a quick guide to help you learn about hair porosity and its impact on hair health.

What is Hair Porosity?

Hair porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. It is influenced by the structure of your hair cuticle and can vary between low, medium, and high porosity.

Types of Hair Porosity

  • Low Porosity: Tightly packed cuticles that resist moisture absorption but retain it well.
  • Medium Porosity: Slightly raised cuticles that balance moisture absorption and retention.
  • High Porosity: Highly raised or damaged cuticles that absorb and lose moisture easily.

How to Determine Your Hair Porosity

  • Float Test: Place a clean strand of hair in a glass of water. Observe if it floats, sinks slowly, or sinks quickly.
  • Slip Test: Slide your fingers up a strand of hair to feel for smoothness or bumps.

Hair Porosity and Care Tips

  • Low Porosity: Use lightweight, water-based products and incorporate heat to help products penetrate.
  • Medium Porosity: Maintain a balanced routine with hydration and protein treatments.
  • High Porosity: Focus on sealing moisture with heavier products and use protein treatments to strengthen your hair.

General Hair Health Tips

  • Avoid excessive heat styling and chemical treatments.
  • Use sulfate-free shampoos to retain natural oils.
  • Regularly deep-condition to maintain moisture.
  • Trim split ends to prevent further damage.
  • Protect hair from environmental factors like UV rays and humidity.
